What is omstilling?

November 15th, 2015

Omstilling is a norwegian word that roughly translates into "realignment". It has been bandied around in Norway quite a lot the last few years for a couple of related reasons. The most immediate has been the recent, dramatic drop in oil prices. Oil and gas make up roughly 30% of the Norwegian economy, so this is bound to have an effect.

A more longterm challenge is the climate change. Norway is western europe's largest petroleum producer. For a people who otherwise would like to think that they place a high value on the environment, Norway's reliance on oil and gas is an uncomfortable fact.

Norway, it is said, needs to change. It needs a more diverse economy, less reliant on oil and gas. A realignment.

This website is meant as as a source of up-to-date data on this realignment in real time. The blog section gives an occasional update and analysis on the state of the economy and country.

But more important is the data section. The goal is to keep an up-to-date, easy to access visual overview of economic and national trends.
